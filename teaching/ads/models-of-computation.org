Big-$O$, or $\mathcal O$ notation relates the asymptotic growth of two functions
to each other.

#+begin_definition Big-O
Given two functions $f, g: \mathbb R \to \mathbb R^{\geq 0}$, we say that
$$f(x) = O(g(x)), \text{ or } f(x)\in O(g(x))$$
when there exists a constant $M\in \mathbb R$ such that
$$ |f(x)| \leq M\cdot g(x) \quad \forall x\in \mathbb R. $$
#+end_definition
